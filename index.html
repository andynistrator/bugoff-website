The Science of Smarter Protection

Our formulation is backed by rigorous clinical data to repel biting bugs while remaining safe for sensitive skin.

Host Receptor Blocker

Bugoff utilizes Icaridin, an advanced active ingredient that acts as a Host Receptor Blocker. It effectively masks human scent signatures, making you "invisible" to biting insects.

Broad-Spectrum Defense:

Proven effective against Mosquitoes, Ticks, Sand Flies, Fleas, and various other biting bugs. This powerful barrier effectively helps prevent insect-borne diseases such as Dengue, Malaria, Zika, and Lyme disease.

Peer-Reviewed Research

Official scientific studies verifying the efficacy and skin-safety profile of our active ingredients.

Independent international specification verifying Icaridin as a highly effective and safe public health repellent, specifically optimized for long-duration use in tropical climates.

World Health Organization (WHO). "Specifications and evaluations for public health pesticides: Icaridin." Pesticide Evaluation Scheme (WHOPES). Source: WHO IRIS (2005)

Comprehensive technical review confirming Picaridin's non-toxic profile, very low skin absorption, and its unique ability to block insect sensors without irritating human skin.

National Pesticide Information Center (NPIC). "Picaridin Technical Fact Sheet." Oregon State University. Source: NPIC Technical Data

Clinical research identifying Lemon Eucalyptus oil (Eucalyptus citriodora) as a powerful botanical agent providing significant protection times against biting mosquitoes.

Trigg, J. K. "Evaluation of a eucalyptus-based repellent against Anopheles mosquitoes." Phytotherapy Research / PubMed. Source: PubMed ID 34917538 (2021)

A scientific review of essential oils confirming that Citrus-derived oils, including Lemon, possess aromatic compounds that effectively disrupt insect questing behaviors.

Nerio, L. S., et al. "Repellent activity of essential oils: a review." Journal of Bioresource Technology / NIH. Source: PMC3059459 (2010)

A clinical review documenting Aloe Vera’s pharmaceutical benefits, specifically its ability to moisturize skin, promote healing, and prevent topical inflammation.

Surjushe, A., et al. "Aloe Vera: A Short Review." Indian Journal of Dermatology / NIH. Source: PMC2763764 (2008)

Official pediatric guidelines recommending Picaridin as a safe, effective, and non-irritating alternative to DEET for infants and children to prevent insect bites.

"Choosing an Insect Repellent for Your Child." American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP). Source: AAP / HealthyChildren (2023)
